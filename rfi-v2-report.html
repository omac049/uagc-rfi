Executive Summary & Methodology

Purpose & Scope

The Request for Information (RFI) form is UAGC's primary lead-generation tool‚Äîthe critical first touchpoint where prospective students express interest in our programs. Every submission represents a potential enrollment, making form optimization directly tied to revenue.

This analysis examines: How four leading online universities structure their RFI forms to maximize completion rates, data quality, and lead conversion.

Methodology

Research Approach

Competitive Set Selection: We analyzed four universities based on:

  • Large online enrollment (50,000+ students)
  • Direct competitors in adult/online education market
  • Established digital marketing presence
  • Known for lead generation excellence

Research-Backed Solutions

1. Multi-Step Form Design

üìä Research Finding

Multi-step forms with one question per step convert 86% higher than single-page forms with many questions.

Why It Works:

  • Reduces cognitive load - Users see only what's needed now
  • Creates momentum - Completing Step 1 commits users to Step 2
  • Lowers anxiety - Clear progress indication shows finish line
  • Allows progressive disclosure - Conditional logic without overwhelming

RFI 2.0 Implementation:

Step 1: Program Selection (2-3 fields)

  • Education Level (Undergraduate vs. Graduate)
  • Select Your Degree (dynamically filtered by level)
  • Conditional: RN License (if nursing program selected)

Step 2: Personal Information (5-6 fields)

  • First Name, Last Name
  • Email, Phone
  • State
  • Military Status

2. Education Level First Approach

üìä Research Finding

Users struggle with lists over 10 items. Level-based filtering improves selection speed by 37% and immediately reduces cognitive load.

Step 1: Select Education Level (2 Simple Options)

1. Undergraduate (Associate's & Bachelor's Degrees)

  • ~45 programs total
  • Entry-level and career advancement
  • AA, BA, BS degrees

2. Graduate (Master's & Doctoral Degrees)

  • ~30 programs total
  • Advanced specialization
  • MA, MS, MBA, M.Accountancy, MPH degrees

  • ‚úÖ Better marketing segmentation
  • ‚úÖ Faster selection (users know their level immediately)
  • ‚úÖ Simpler choice architecture (2 options ‚Üí specific list)

UAGC RFI 2.0 Strategy & Recommendations

üéØ Strategic Direction

Based on comprehensive competitive analysis of SNHU, WGU, Walden, and Purdue Global, this section outlines specific recommendations for UAGC's RFI 2.0 redesign.

Recommended Form Architecture

Two-Step Wizard Design (Adopted from WGU/Purdue)

Why: 50% of competitors use multi-step design. Research shows 86% higher conversion vs. single-page.

Step 1: Program Selection (2-3 fields)

  • Education Level radio buttons: "Undergraduate" or "Graduate"
  • Select Your Program dropdown (dynamically filtered by level)
  • Conditional: RN License question (appears only for nursing programs)

Step 2: Personal Information (5-6 fields)

  • First Name, Last Name
  • Email, Phone
  • State (dropdown)
  • Military Status (4 options: Active Duty, Veteran, Spouse/Dependent, No)
  • Optional: Start Timeline ("When do you plan to start?")

‚úÖ Best-of-All-Worlds Approach

This combines:

  • WGU's low-friction Step 1 (minimal fields)
  • SNHU's hierarchical filtering (level ‚Üí program)
  • Walden's conditional logic (RN question)
  • Purdue's start timeline (lead scoring)
